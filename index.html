About Me

I have an analytical mind and a voracious appetite for learning new skills. I am a natural problem solver and critical thinker. I am interested in both data and policy, and I have mathematical skills as well as writing and communication skills.

My background is in Criminal Justice and Addictions Counseling. I worked as a Researcher in Graduate School and at Multnomah County Corrections Health for two years prior to becoming a counselor.

The pandemic demonstrated the power and importance of data and also inspired me to make a career change. Now I have a fresh set of newly-aquired skills in data analytics, including Excel, SQL, Tableau and R. I am looking forward to developing these skills further through employment as a full-time data analyst.
